## Current disposition and proposed historical-pilot amendment

Under the currently approved plan, the incomplete sequential-keyboard, actual-zoom, safe-area, reduced-motion, saved-history reload, and active-resume evidence keeps TREK-206 blocked. Defects are valid pilot findings, but they do not substitute for required missing evidence.

A bounded amendment has been proposed but is not approved: for this frozen historical replay only, a row that cannot be safely and faithfully exercised could close as a classified limitation when the matrix records the attempted method, why it is unsafe or unfaithful, residual risk, and an explicit `inconclusive`, `not-tested`, or `static-risk` result. That amendment would permit completion of the pilot's evidence-cost and defect-discovery objective, not a usability pass or product acceptance. It would create no authority for EPIC-8 changes or future tasks; future material UI work would remain blocked until its required evidence is observed unless the user explicitly approves a task-specific exception before implementation-plan approval. The proposed amendment requires design review, user approval, planning-conformance review, and updated Trekker intent before it can govern completion.
